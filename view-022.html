◈ PROTOCOL 01: SYMPATHETIC MAGIC - OVERVIEW ◈

Frazer identifies magic principles that resolve into TWO fundamental laws:

1. LAW OF SIMILARITY "Like produces like" Effect resembles cause 2. LAW OF CONTACT "Things once in contact act at a distance" Physical severing does not end influence
EFFECT ────→ CAUSE ↑ ↑ │ │ SYMBOLIC MATERIAL REALM REALM │ │ └────────────┘ SYMPATHETIC LINK

These are the foundational principles of all sympathetic magic systems across cultures.

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 20a.

◈ SUB-PROTOCOL 01.1: LAW OF SIMILARITY ◈

The magician infers that any effect can be produced by IMITATING IT.

This is called Homoeopathic Magic or Imitative Magic.

REFLECTION ═══ ORIGINAL What happens to the reflection ↓ Happens to the original

The principle: Mimicry becomes causality. The similarity between symbol and thing makes the symbol functionally equivalent to the thing.

Example logic: "I can burn this drawing of my enemy → His body burns."

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 20a.

◈ CASE STUDY: HOSTILE IMITATION - OJIBWAY ◈

ACTION: Destroy enemy by injuring its image.

METHOD: Ojibway make wooden image of foe → insert needle in head/heart → foe feels pain/dies.

WOODEN IMAGE ┌─────────────┐ │ ●● │ ← Eyes │ ▼ │ ← Needle inserted │ ▪ ▪ ▪ ▪ │ └─────────────┘ ↓ ORIGINAL PERSON FEELS PAIN AT SAME LOCATION

The sympathetic link is based on similarity: the wooden figure resembles the person enough to be functionally identical for magical purposes.

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 22a.

◈ SUB-PROTOCOL 01.2: LAW OF CONTACT ◈

Whatever is done to a material object affects the PERSON ONCE IN CONTACT WITH IT.

This is called Contagious Magic.

PERSON ════════ OBJECT │ hair │ │ nails │ │ blood │ │ spittle │ └─────CUT────────┘ CONTACT SEVERED BUT LINK PERSISTS Act on object → affects person

The principle: Physical contact creates a permanent sympathetic link. Distance and time cannot break it.

Example logic: "I have your hair → I can curse you from 100 miles away."

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 20a.

◈ CASE STUDY: COMBINED MALAY CHARM ◈

HYBRID PROTOCOL: Combine BOTH similarity AND contact magic for maximum effect.

INGREDIENTS: - Wax effigy (SIMILARITY) - Victim's hair (CONTACT) - Victim's nails (CONTACT) - Victim's spittle (CONTACT) PROCEDURE: Insert materials into wax figure Scorch over lamp INCANTATION: "It is not wax... It is liver, heart, spleen..."
┌──────────────────┐ │ WAX FIGURE │ │ + HAIR │ │ + NAILS │ ← HOMOEOPATHIC │ + SPITTLE │ ← CONTAGIOUS │ │ └─────────┬────────┘ │ SCORCH │ ↓ VICTIM BURNS (at a distance)

The combination of both principles maximizes magical effect: the figure LOOKS like the person (similarity) AND contains their biological material (contact).

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 22a-22b.

◈ PROTOCOL 02: THEORETICAL vs. PRACTICAL MAGIC ◈

Frazer distinguishes between two ways of approaching magic:

THEORETICAL MAGIC: - Magic as "spurious system of natural law" - Magic as pseudo-science - Magic as flawed explanation of causality PRACTICAL MAGIC: - Magic as "set of precepts" - Magic as ritual procedures - Magic as technique for achieving ends

Key insight: Primitive practitioners know magic ONLY on its practical side. They perform rituals that "work" (produce desired outcomes) without understanding the theoretical mechanism.

Magic is ART, NEVER SCIENCE - effective practice based on false theory.

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 20a-20b.

◈ PROTOCOL 02.1: THE FUNDAMENTAL FALLACY ◈

Frazer identifies magic as a "misapplication of the association of ideas."

HOMOEOPATHIC FALLACY: Assuming things which RESEMBLE each other ARE FUNCTIONALLY THE SAME CONTAGIOUS FALLACY: Assuming things once in CONTACT ARE ALWAYS IN CONTACT (even after physical separation)
COGNITIVE ERROR: Look similar? ↓ → MUST be identical → Acting on symbol = acting on thing This is WRONG (but feels inevitable)

Magic works because humans are pattern-recognition machines prone to specific cognitive errors. We conflate similarity with identity, and assume contact creates permanent bonds.

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 21b.

◈ PROTOCOL 03: BENEVOLENT IMITATION - FERTILITY ◈

Sympathetic magic is not always hostile. Positive applications use the same principles to CREATE rather than DESTROY.

BATAKS (Sumatra): Barren woman holds WOODEN IMAGE OF CHILD to conceive DYAKS (Borneo): Wizard mimics childbirth movements with stone representing baby
IMITATE DESIRED STATE Want: Child ↓ Action: Hold wooden child image ↓ Theory: Similarity causes reality ↓ Result: (desired) Pregnancy

The same principle of sympathetic magic: make the desired outcome REAL through imitation. If you mime childbirth, birth will follow.

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 23a.

◈ PROTOCOL 03.1: BENEVOLENT IMITATION - HEALING ◈

Color-based healing uses sympathetic magic to transfer properties.

HINDUS (jaundice cure): - Banish yellow (jaundice) to yellow BIRDS - Infuse red (health) from RED BULL - Transfer color-property via similarity STONECURLEW CURE: - Bird has LARGE GOLDEN EYES - Golden property draws out yellow jaundice - Visual similarity = functional equivalence
PATIENT with jaundice (yellow) ↓ YELLOW BIRD (similar property) ↓ Jaundice transfers to bird ↓ PATIENT healed (red/healthy)

The principle: properties are transferable through sympathetic correspondence. Red transfers health, yellow transfers disease, etc.

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 24a-24b.

◈ PROTOCOL 03.2: PROXY HEALING - DOCTOR AS MIRROR ◈

The healer mimics the patient's condition to transfer illness OUT of patient.

PERCHE (France): Doctor contorts own stomach Patient's stomach pain ceases DYAK SHAMAN: Medicine-man pretends to DIE Sick person recovers MECHANISM: Doctor becomes sympathetic COPY of patient Illness transfers to copy Original patient heals
PATIENT (sick) ↓ DOCTOR (mimics symptoms) ↓ Sympathetic similarity created ↓ ILLNESS TRANSFERS to doctor (as sympathetic copy) ↓ PATIENT HEALS

The doctor functions as a sympathetic substitute through imitation of symptoms.

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 25a.

◈ PROTOCOL 04: RESOURCE GENERATION - TOTEMS ◈

Aboriginal Australian peoples use sympathetic magic to multiply food resources.

CENTRAL AUSTRALIA: Each totem clan responsible for multiplying their totem animal/plant (e.g., witchetty grubs) ARUNTA PRACTICE: Clan performs ceremonial pantomime of grubs emerging from ground Grubs increase in number in region
WANT: More grubs ↓ MIME: Grubs emerging ↓ PERFORM: Ritual repetition ↓ RESULT: Grubs multiply (sympathetic imitation causes reality)

Ceremonial imitation of desired abundance creates actual abundance through sympathetic correspondence.

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 26a.

◈ PROTOCOL 04.1: SYMPATHETIC HUNTING MAGIC ◈

Hunters use sympathetic magic to guarantee successful catch.

NOOTKA (British Columbia): Wizard makes FISH IMAGE Places it in water Real fish attracted to image Catch guaranteed CAMBODIAN HUNTER: Simulates being CAUGHT IN NET before actual hunting Real catch follows
METHOD 1: SIMILARITY Fish image in water ↓ Attracts real fish (image is sympathetic lure) METHOD 2: IMITATION Hunter caught in net (mimics) ↓ Real fish caught in net (imitation precedes reality)

Both methods: use sympathetic correspondence to guarantee desired outcome (catch) by preceding it with image/imitation.

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 26b.

◈ PROTOCOL 05: NEGATIVE MAGIC - TABOO ◈

Taboos are the NEGATIVE pole of sympathetic magic.

Magic: "DO THIS" (sorcery - positive imitation)

Taboo: "DO NOT DO THIS, lest [bad outcome] occur"

TABOO is PREVENTIVE magic based on same principles of similarity and contact If you want to PREVENT an outcome, avoid actions that RESEMBLE it
TABOO LOGIC: Want to prevent: Entanglement ↓ Avoid: Actions that resemble entanglement ↓ Therefore: Don't play Cat's Cradle (fingers tangling in string resembles harpoon line entanglement)

Taboos prevent undesired outcomes through NEGATIVE imitation - avoiding actions that resemble bad results.

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 27a-27b.

◈ CASE STUDY: TABOO - CAT'S CRADLE ◈

THE RULE: Esquimaux boys forbidden to play Cat's Cradle.

THE RATIONALE: Fingers might later become entangled in harpoon line during hunting.

STRING GAME (Cat's Cradle) ↓ Fingers tangled in string ↓ SIMILARITY ↓ Must avoid, or: ↓ Fingers WILL tangle in harpoon line (during hunting) ↓ Death

MECHANISM: Similarity-based avoidance. Fingers tangled in play = fingers tangled in real hunting = failure and death.

Prevent the imitation, prevent the reality.

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 27b.

◈ CASE STUDY: TABOO - SPINNING ◈

THE RULE: Huzul (Carpathians) wives may NOT SPIN while husband eats.

THE RATIONALE: Game will "turn and wind like the spindle" - hunter will miss.

WIFE SPINS (spindle rotating) ↓ SIMILARITY ↓ GAME TWISTS (spins like spindle) ↓ HUNTER MISSES (can't catch spinning prey) ↓ STARVATION

MECHANISM: Similarity-based prevention. Spinning motion in home CAUSES spinning motion in game CAUSES hunting failure.

The wife's action is a sympathetic simulation that produces real hunting consequences.

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 27b.

◈ FRAZER'S CONCLUSION ◈

Magic is:

"One great disastrous fallacy, a mistaken conception of the ASSOCIATION OF IDEAS"

Magic divides into two opposed poles:

SORCERY (positive) TABOO (negative) "Do this" "Don't do this" ↓ ↓ Imitate desired outcome Avoid resembling bad outcome ↓ ↓ Force reality Prevent reality BOTH BASED ON SAME FALLACY: Similarity creates causality Contact creates permanent bonds

Both magic and taboo are "vain and futile" - they work psychologically (placebo/nocebo) but not actually.

Yet they generate observable behavior and structure entire societies.

SOURCE: Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 27b.

You now understand sympathetic magic. Apply this framework to digital systems:

DIGITAL AVATAR = SYMPATHETIC IMAGE - Avatar resembles person (similarity) - Data from person embedded in avatar (contact) - Acting on avatar affects person DEEPFAKE = HOSTILE SYMPATHETIC MAGIC - Image resembles target (similarity) - Behavioral imitation (contact/contagion) - False image causes real harm DATA PROFILE = CONTAGIOUS CONTACT - Aggregated from your traces (hair, nails) - Continues to affect you at distance - "Acting on profile" = real consequences SOCIAL MEDIA = SYMPATHETIC RESONANCE - Profile must resemble "real you" (similarity) - Engagement creates sympathetic bond (contact) - What you simulate becomes real (Frazer's principle)

ETHICAL QUESTION:

If Frazer's principles are "fallacies," why do they generate such real consequences in digital systems?

ANSWER: Because belief structures reality. Sympathetic magic "works" not as physics but as cultural technology that makes people ACT as if it works.
