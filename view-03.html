SEC-01 / MISSION BRIEF
MISSION BRIEF
PRIMARY QUESTION

How do sympathetic magic's principles shape the ethics of representing <memory>, <identity>, and <community> in media?

KEY CONSTRUCT

<representation> creates linkages between form and <referent> that persist through <archives> and <networks>

OPERATIONAL FRAME

Two principles govern these linkages:
[similarity] — like affects like
[association] — contact leaves trace

SCOPE

This field manual maps ethical risks and duties across three domains of media practice, provides operational heuristics, and offers audit tools for practitioners.

SEC-02 / PRINCIPLES
PRINCIPLES

[SIMILARITY]

LAW: "Like affects like"

Form, style, and voice [transfer] qualities to <referent>

MECHANISM

Visual/sonic/textual resemblance creates perceived equivalence. Repeated patterns strengthen association.

EXAMPLE RISKS:
  • → Stereotypes [stick] to groups
  • → Voice casting [imputes] identity
  • → Documentary style [transfers] authority

[ASSOCIATION]

LAW: "Contact leaves a trace"

Past [contact] persists through <archives> and <networks>

MECHANISM

Metadata, provenance, and context create durable connections. Digital systems amplify propagation.

EXAMPLE RISKS:
  • → Archival images [retrigger] trauma
  • → Dataset reuse [propagates] bias
  • → Metadata [traces] persist
SEC-03 / DOMAIN: MEMORY
DOMAIN: <MEMORY>

RISKS

  • [overwrite] — replacement of lived experience with mediated version
  • [sanitize] — removal of complexity/context
  • [mislead] — false equivalence through resemblance
  • [retrigger] — reactivation of trauma through archival contact

DUTIES

  • [contextualize] — preserve full provenance and framing
  • [consent-seek] — ongoing permission from stakeholders
  • [trace] — maintain lineage and attribution
  • [slow-down] — add friction to prevent rapid propagation

Portraits and Dreams

Wendy Ewald, 2000
<memory> + <identity>

Collaborative photography project with children in North Carolina. Combines their photographs with their written dreams.

Risks [similarity]: Photographs directly [transfer] qualities of childhood, community to viewers; risk of [sanitize] lived experience
Risks [association]: [contact] between image and child persists; reuse of images [propagates] downstream
Duties Applied: [consent-seek] through ongoing collaboration, [contextualize] with children's own narratives, [trace] provenance
Outcome: Shifted paradigm from extractive documentation to co-authored representation
Image source: Library of Congress
SEC-04 / DOMAIN: IDENTITY
DOMAIN: <IDENTITY>

RISKS

  • [impute] stereotypes — assign group traits to individual
  • [adhere] labels — sticky categorization
  • [bleed] group to person — categorical collapse
  • [stick] stigma — durable negative association

DUTIES

  • [co-author] — shared narrative authority
  • [disaggregate] — separate individual from group
  • [minimize] identifiers — reduce unnecessary markers
  • [audit-casting] — check representation choices

Voice Casting Ethics

Industry Guidelines, 2023
<identity>

Animation and voiceover production guidelines addressing authentic casting vs. labor accessibility.

Risks [similarity]: [similarity] between voice and character identity creates [stick] associations; can [impute] stereotypes
Risks [association]: Voice actors [adhere] to archived performances; casting choices [bleed] into cultural perception
Duties Applied: [co-author] casting decisions with affected communities, [disaggregate] voice from identity markers, [minimize] identifiers
Outcome: Moving toward transparency in casting rationale and consent protocols
Image source: Wikimedia Commons (CC0)
SEC-05 / DOMAIN: COMMUNITY
DOMAIN: <COMMUNITY>

RISKS

  • [stand-in] — individual represents whole group
  • [flatten] — collapse internal diversity
  • [cluster] — forced taxonomic grouping
  • [stigmatize] — negative group marking

DUTIES

  • [pluralize] — show multiple perspectives
  • [reciprocate] — mutual benefit exchange
  • [govern] taxonomies — community control over categories
  • [steward] long-term data — ongoing care

Stranger with a Camera

Elizabeth Barret, 1999
<community>

Documentary about outsider filmmakers in Appalachia. Explores power dynamics when strangers document communities.

Risks [similarity]: [similarity] between documentary tropes and stereotype templates [stick] labels to region
Risks [association]: Archive of footage becomes [trace] that [cluster] community identity; reuse creates lasting harm
Duties Applied: [audit-casting] of narrative frame, [pluralize] voices, [reciprocate] through community agency, [steward] archive
Outcome: Meta-documentary examining ethical dimensions of representation itself
Image source: PBS Distribution

SEC-07 / HEURISTICS
HEURISTICS

Four "Shop Standards" for operational guidance:

LIKENESS IS LEVERAGE

When [similarity] increases, apply [co-author], [contextualize], [audit-casting]

Guidance: The more closely representation resembles referent, the stronger the ethical duty to involve stakeholders and preserve context.

TRACES TRAVEL

When radius of [association] expands, add [trace], [limit-propagation], [steward]

Guidance: As reach grows through networks and archives, increase provenance tracking and propagation controls.

COUNTER-LIKENESS PROTECTS

[pluralize] with counter-examples

Guidance: Break stereotype patterns by showing diverse instances. Multiple representations weaken singular [stick] effects.

CONSENT SCALES WITH STICKINESS

Strengthen [consent-seek] and [repair] as associative persistence rises

Guidance: The more durable and propagable the representation, the more robust consent and repair protocols must be.
