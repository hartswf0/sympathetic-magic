PROTOCOL 01: SYMPATHETIC MAGIC - OVERVIEW

Frazer: Magic principles resolve into two:

1. LIKE PRODUCES LIKE (Effect resembles cause).

2. THINGS ONCE IN CONTACT ACT AT A DISTANCE (Physical contact severed, influence persists).

THEORIES OF REMOTE ACTION. OBSERVE INTERCONNECTEDNESS.

SOURCE: Wikimedia Commons (PD-Art). Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 20a.

SUB-PROTOCOL 01.1: LAW OF SIMILARITY (IMITATIVE MAGIC)

Magician infers he can produce any effect by IMITATING IT.

This is Homoeopathic or Imitative Magic.

MIMICRY AS CAUSALITY. REFLECTION IS ACTION. (Dirck van Baburen, PD-Art)

SOURCE: Wikimedia Commons (PD-Art). Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 20a.

CASE STUDY: HOSTILE IMITATION - OJIBWAY

DESTROY ENEMY by injuring its image.

Ojibway: make wooden image, stick needle in head/heart = foe feels pain.

EFFIGY. TARGET ACQUIRED. DIRECT DAMAGE TRANSLATION. (Wellcome V0046537, PD-Art)

SOURCE: Wellcome Collection (PD-Art). Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 22a.

SUB-PROTOCOL 01.2: LAW OF CONTACT (CONTAGIOUS MAGIC)

Whatever is done to a material object affects the PERSON ONCE IN CONTACT.

This is Contagious Magic.

BIOLOGICAL DATA. PHYSICAL CONTACT FOR REMOTE EFFECT. (SEM Microscopy, PD)

SOURCE: Wikimedia Commons (CC0). Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 20a.

CASE STUDY: COMBINED MALAY CHARM

Wax effigy + victim's hair/nails/spittle.

Scorch figure over lamp: "IT IS NOT WAX... IT IS LIVER, HEART, SPLEEN..."

HOMOEOPATHIC + CONTAGIOUS = maximized effect.

HYBRID ASSAULT PROTOCOL. OBSERVATIONAL DATA. (Wellcome V0010156, PD-Art)

SOURCE: Wellcome Collection (CC-BY 4.0). Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 22a-22b.

PROTOCOL 02: THEORETICAL vs. PRACTICAL MAGIC

Theoretical Magic: Magic as "spurious system of natural law."

Practical Magic: Magic as "set of precepts" to achieve ends.

Primitive practitioners know magic only on its PRACTICAL SIDE. It is ART, NEVER SCIENCE.

ART VS. SCIENCE. FLAWED LOGIC. PSEUDO-DISCIPLINE. (Pieter Bruegel d. Ä., 1558, PD-Art)

SOURCE: Wikimedia Commons (PD-Art). Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 20a-20b.

PROTOCOL 02.1: THE FALLACY - ASSOCIATION OF IDEAS

Frazer: Magic is a "misapplication of the association of ideas."

HOMOEOPATHIC: mistake of assuming things which resemble each other ARE THE SAME.

CONTAGIOUS: mistake of assuming things once in contact ARE ALWAYS IN CONTACT.

COGNITIVE ERROR DETECTED. CORRELATION ≠ CAUSATION. (Historical Phrenology, PD)

SOURCE: Wikimedia Commons (PD-Old). Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 21b.

Bataks (Sumatra): Barren woman holds WOODEN IMAGE OF CHILD to conceive.

Dyaks (Borneo): Wizard mimics labor movements with a stone representing baby.

REPLICATING LIFE. PROTOTYPE FOR FERTILITY. (Akua'ba, Asante people, PD-Art)

PROTOCOL 03.1: BENEVOLENT IMITATION - HEALING

Hindus (jaundice): Banish yellow to yellow birds, infuse red from RED BULL.

Stonecurlew: Draws out yellow jaundice via its LARGE GOLDEN EYE.

COLOR TRANSFER. BIO-REMEDIATION. AFFECT SIMILARLY. (Nandi Bull, PD-Art)

PROTOCOL 03.2: BENEVOLENT IMITATION - PROXY HEALING

Perche (France): Doctor contorts to "unhook" his own stomach; patient relieved.

Dyak: Medicine-man pretends to die; sick person recovers.

SUBSTITUTIONAL ANGUISH. THE DOCTOR AS MIRROR. (Wellcome V0015505, PD-Art)

REPLICA FOR ABUNDANCE. RITUAL REPLICATION. (Witchetty grub, PD)

Cambodian hunter: Simulates being caught in his own net to ensure real catch.

PROTOCOL 05: NEGATIVE MAGIC - TABOO

Prohibitions (Taboos) based on LAWS OF SIMILARITY & CONTACT.

Magic: "DO THIS" (Sorcery).

Taboo: "DO NOT DO THIS, lest so and so should happen."

UNDESIRABLE EVENT AVOIDANCE. WARNING. (SVG Vector, CC0)

SOURCE: Wikimedia Commons (CC0). Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 27a-27b.

CASE STUDY: TABOO - CAT'S CRADLE

Esquimaux boys: Forbidden to play CAT'S CRADLE.

WHY? Fingers might later entangle in harpoon line.

SIMILARITY-BASED AVOIDANCE.

INTERFERENCE PROTOCOL. PRE-EMPTIVE MEASURES. (String game, CC0)

SOURCE: Wikimedia Commons (CC0/PD-Old). Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 27b.

CASE STUDY: TABOO - SPINNING

Huzul (Carpathians): Hunter's wife may not SPIN while he eats.

WHY? Game will "turn and wind like the spindle," hunter misses.

Another SIMILARITY-BASED AVOIDANCE.

DIVERGENT ACTION PREVENTION. AVOID UNWANTED EFFECTS. (Historical illustration, PD)

SOURCE: Wikimedia Commons (PD-Old). Frazer, J.G. (1922). The Golden Bough, p. 27b.

FINAL PROTOCOL: FRAZER'S CONCLUSION

Magic: "one great disastrous fallacy, a mistaken conception of the ASSOCIATION OF IDEAS."

SORCERY (positive pole) vs. TABOO (negative pole).

Both are "vain and futile."
